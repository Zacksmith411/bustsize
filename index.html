How to Measure Your Bra Size

1. Measure Underbust (Band Size)

Stand straight and wrap a measuring tape snugly around your ribcage, directly under your bust where the bra band sits. Keep the tape level and comfortable.

Note the measurement in inches. This number is your band size (for example: 32, 34, 36).

2. Measure Bust Size

Wrap the measuring tape around the fullest part of your bust. The tape should be straight across your back and not too tight.

Write down this measurement in inches.

3. Find Your Cup Size

Subtract your underbust measurement from your bust measurement. The difference determines your cup size.

5DD

Example: Underbust: 32 inches, Bust: 36 inches → Difference: 4 inches → Bra Size: 32D

How to Check Proper Bra Fitting

Even if your size is correct, a bra should fit properly in all areas. Here’s how to know if your bra fits well:

✔ Band Fit

  • The band should sit straight across your back.
  • It should feel snug but not tight.
  • You should be able to fit two fingers under the band.

✔ Cup Fit

  • Your breasts should fill the cups without spilling over.
  • No gaps or wrinkling in the cups.
  • No bulging on the sides or top.

✔ Straps

  • Straps should not dig into your shoulders.
  • They should not slip off.
  • Most support should come from the band, not the straps.

✔ Center Gore

  • The center part of the bra should lie flat against your chest.

If you experience discomfort, back pain, or frequent adjustments, your bra size or style may be incorrect.
